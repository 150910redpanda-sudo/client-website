Gibbs Hall Barn is a sympathetic conversion of an old farm building, set within the stunning Cumbrian countryside of the Yorkshire Dales National Park. Original stone walls and high timber-beamed ceilings are paired with every modern comfort.

The spacious open-plan living room — with its roaring fireplace and full-height glass doors framing the valley — becomes the natural heart of every stay. Spacious enough for families, intimate enough for two.

Heritage

Three centuries
in the making

The Gibbs Hall farmstead dates to the late 17th or early 18th century — a Grade II listed complex of sandstone and slate in the heart of Dentdale. The farmhouse, though now a consolidated ruin, retains its chamfered mullion windows and corbelled chimney stack.

The former barn, quietly converted to a dwelling, carries that same spirit of place. Thick stone walls, original proportions and the drama of the surrounding landscape remain unchanged across 300 years.

Explore

The Dales,
the Lakes,
the railway

Leave the car behind for days, or venture further. The Lake District is under 30 miles west. Wensleydale and the Three Peaks are on your eastern horizon. Every direction promises something extraordinary.

01 — On your doorstep

Dent Village

Cobbled streets, two real ale pubs, three cafes, a heritage centre celebrating Adam Sedgwick and a well-stocked village store. Under 3 miles.

02 — 5 miles east

Settle–Carlisle Railway

Dent Station — the highest mainline station in England at 1,150ft. Cross the Arten Gill and Dent Head viaducts. Ride to Carlisle or Leeds for a day out.

03 — Walking

Dales Way & Cycleway

Both the Dales Way footpath and the Yorkshire Dales Cycleway pass through Dent village. Hundreds of miles of trails directly accessible on foot or bike.

04 — Under 30 miles

Lake District National Park

Windermere, Coniston, Ambleside and the southern Lakes are all within easy reach for a spectacular day trip west.

05 — Day trips

White Scar Cave & The Dales

Visit England's longest showcave at Ingleton. Explore Hawes, the Wensleydale Creamery, Sedbergh's independent bookshops and Kirkby Lonsdale.

06 — Wildlife

River Dee & the Valley

Meander beside the Dee spotting heron, dippers and kingfishers. The views from the high fells above Dentdale stretch for miles in every direction.
